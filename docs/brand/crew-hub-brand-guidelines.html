Internal use only. Accrue Technologies Inc. All markets.

"Warmth turned inward. The same cream. The same orange. But quieter, steadier, more structural."

Accrue was built to serve hundreds of thousands of users across Africa. Crew Hub is built to serve the people who build for them. Both deserve the same quality of attention, the same care, the same brand discipline. This is not a secondary system. It is the same system, held closer.

01
Brand Foundation

The crew
at the center.

Crew Hub is Accrue's internal operating layer. Every design decision here starts from one belief: the people building Accrue deserve the same quality of experience as the people using it. Same family. Different room.

Purpose

Give the crew what they give users.

Accrue gives users clarity, warmth, and control over their money. Crew Hub gives the team building Accrue the same clarity, warmth, and control over how they work. The internal experience should match the ambition of the external product.

Positioning

The internal layer of a distributed team.

Crew Hub is where HR, ops, and team coordination live for a cross-border team spanning Lagos, Accra, and beyond. It is not an afterthought. It is infrastructure with a face, and that face should feel as considered as the product it supports.

Direct
blunt

We say what we mean without softening it into confusion. But directness is paired with respect. Information lands clearly because it was shaped with care, not because it was sharpened as a weapon.

Warm
casual

This is a people platform. The tone carries genuine regard for whoever is reading it, whether they are requesting leave or reviewing a policy. Warmth is not decoration. It is function.

Steady
rigid

The crew depends on Crew Hub to be reliable. Consistency in language and visual behaviour builds the kind of trust that lets the team move fast without second-guessing the tools beneath them.

02
Colour

Inherited.
Precise.

Every Crew Hub colour traces directly back to Accrue's confirmed brand DNA. Nothing invented. Two colours extended, carefully, to serve operational states that do not exist in the consumer product.

The palette is an argument, not a decoration.

Cream is warmth at rest. Orange is precision in action. Black is where important things live. The two extensions, Navy and Sage, exist only because internal tools need states (authority, approval) that consumer apps handle differently. Every colour earns its place or does not appear.

04
Voice & Tone

Words that
sound like us.

Voice is the character. Tone is how the character shows up depending on context. Crew Hub has one voice, four principles, and infinite tonal range between warm and direct depending on what the moment calls for.

1
Direct
not blunt

Say what you mean in the fewest precise words. No corporate hedging. No softening into vagueness. But directness here is shaped with enough care that it lands as help, not a command.

2
Warm
not casual

There are people behind every request in this system. The voice carries genuine regard for them, whether they are requesting leave or reviewing payroll. Warmth is not decoration here. It is infrastructure.

3
Clear
not simple

Clear language treats the reader as intelligent. It does not dumb down. It strips away complexity that exists for the writer's benefit rather than the reader's. The crew is smart. Write accordingly.

4
Crew-first
not system-first

The language always centres the person using the tool, not the tool itself. "Your request has been submitted" not "The system has logged your input." The crew is the subject. Everything else is supporting cast.

07
Do's & Don'ts

The rules,
shown.

Every rule here exists because someone will eventually face the situation. These are not restrictions. They are the places where thinking has already been done so the team does not have to do it again.

Colour Usage
Do
Use Orange for the primary CTA and let it stand alone.
Orange earns its power through scarcity. One anchor per screen creates hierarchy. Multiple oranges create noise.
Don't
Use orange for buttons, headings, borders, AND highlights on the same screen.
When everything shouts, nothing is heard. Orange used carelessly becomes background noise.
Typography
Do
Use serif for page titles and key moments. Use sans for UI elements and body text.
The contrast between the two typefaces creates visual hierarchy. Each has its domain. Respect it.
Don't
Mix both typefaces within the same UI component, or use serif for form labels.
Typeface mixing within a single element creates visual noise and signals disorder, not creativity.
Voice
Do
Your leave request has been approved. 10 days will be added back to your balance.
Crew-first. Clear. Tells the person what happened AND what it means for them. That is useful communication.
Don't
The leave management system has successfully processed your leave application request and updated your records accordingly.
System-first. Passive. The tool is talking about itself. Cut the preamble. Say the thing.
Layout
Do
Use white space deliberately. Let cards breathe on the Cream canvas. Groups of related items should cluster; unrelated items should have room between them.
Generous spacing communicates that content is organised and that the team behind the tool cares about clarity.
Don't
Pack every available pixel with content. Add more cards to a dashboard just because there is space. Fill silence with noise.
A crowded layout communicates urgency and disorder. Crew Hub should feel calm and in control at all times.
For AI Agents

This guide is your brief.

Any AI agent working on Crew Hub interfaces, copy, communications, or documentation must follow this guide. This section is the canonical reference for agents. Internalize it. Apply it. Every output that carries the Crew Hub name should be indistinguishable from something built by a designer who has lived in this system.

Colour

Default canvas is #FFFAF3 Cream. Orange #FD8B05 appears once per screen as the primary CTA, never more. Status colours are restricted to their chip contexts. Do not introduce colours outside this system.

Typography

Headlines and openers use Playfair Display. All UI, labels, body, and buttons use DM Sans. Body text minimum is 13px. Labels are always uppercase, tracked. Never mix typefaces within a single element.

Voice

Crew-first language always. Use "crew" not "staff". Use "send a request" not "submit a ticket". Cut corporate hedging. No em dashes. Sentences are shorter than you think they need to be. Say the thing.

Components

Buttons: Primary is Black, CTA is Orange (one per screen). Cards: White on Cream, 1px #E8DFD0 border, 12px radius. Form focus: Orange border. Required fields: asterisk in Orange.

Spacing

Never crowd elements. Cards have 24px internal padding. Groups of related items sit 16px apart. Unrelated sections have at least 48px between them. White space is not wasted space. It is how Crew Hub breathes.

Extension colours

Navy #1A2B3C is for navigation and admin states only. Sage #2D6A4F is for approval and success states only. Neither appears as a general brand colour. Do not expand their usage without explicit instruction.

Pre-output Checklist for Agents

Canvas is Cream (#FFFAF3). White surfaces sit on it. Not the other way around.
Orange appears once as the primary CTA. Not twice. Not as a text colour in body copy.
Serif typeface is used for headlines only, not UI labels, buttons, or body text.
Language is crew-first. The person is the subject, not the system.
No em dashes appear anywhere in the output.
No corporate jargon: no "leverage", no "synergize", no "please be advised".
Status colours are only used within chip components, paired with their designated tint background.
Every button is labelled with a verb that tells the person what will happen when they click it.
Spacing is generous. If it feels crowded, remove something rather than shrinking it.
The Accrue wordmark is never used without the Crew Hub sub-label in this context.
Closing

The crew
notices.

Every time this brand is applied consistently, it earns trust. Every shortcut taken, every colour introduced without reason, every button labelled with a vague word instead of a clear verb, costs trust. The people using Crew Hub are the same people building the product that hundreds of thousands of Africans depend on. They deserve tools that reflect that seriousness. This guide exists so no one has to guess what serious looks like.
